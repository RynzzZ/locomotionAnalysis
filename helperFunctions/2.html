Section 1: Part 1
1) Which of the following is FALSE?
A) Minor surgeries do not require aseptic technique
B) Multiple major survival surgeries need to be scientifically justified and approved by the IACUC
C) At a minimum the surgical site should be clipped, the surgeon should wear gloves, and the instruments and surrounding area should be clean for non-survival surgery
D) General principles of aseptic technique should be followed for all survival surgeries
2) Which of the following is a recommended heat source according to ICM?
A) Circulating warm water blanket
B) Heat lamp
C) Electric heating pad
3) T/F Incisions should be made parallel to tension lines.
A) True
B) False
4) Which of the following is FALSE regarding body temperature of rodents during surgery?
A) Rodents have faster heat loss compared to large animals because of their high body surface area:volume ratio
B) Surgical time has no effect on anesthetic recovery
C) Intra-operative heat loss decreases anesthetic metabolism and urinary excretion of anesthetics
5) Regarding patient preparation which of the following is FALSE?
A) Clean gloves should be worn to scrub the animals skin
B) Hair does not need to be trimmed if the incision will be <0.5cm in length
C) Clipping should be performed in a location separate from the designated surgery area, or minimally on a drape that can be removed and discarded
D) Any clipped hair or debris should be removed from the surgical area
6) Which of the following is not a factor affecting asepsis?
A) Time of surgical procedure
B) Trauma sustained by tissue
C) Anesthetic choice
D) Contamination of wound with bacteria or foreign material
7) Which of the following is FALSE regarding suturing?
A) Incisions that enter a body cavity require multiple tissue layer closure (muscle and skin)
B) Incisions that are <1cm in length require only skin to be closed
C) Inner tissue layer (muscle) must be sutured with absorbable suture material
D) Outer tissue layer (skin) should be sutured with monofilament (non-braided) suture material
8) T/F A mouse is anesthetized for surgery and is allowed to recover from anesthesia following the surgery for 1 hour in order to collect data. After this hour it is euthanized. This is an example of a non-survival surgery.
A) True
B) False
9) Which of the following is considered a minor surgery as per the USDA and Guide for the Care and Use of Laboratory Animals?
A) Craniotomy
B) Limb amputation
C) Subcutaneous osmotic pump placement
D) Thoracotomy
10) T/F Rodents must not be fed prior to surgery.
A) True
B) False
